Cross-Platform Talking Points

Speaker reference card — opening framing + in-class call-out moments to broaden the IG playbook to other platforms without changing the deck.

Speaker only · do not distribute
How to use this card: Glance at it before each section. The opening script gives you the framing for the first 90 seconds of class so agents don't think you're ignoring TikTok/YouTube/LinkedIn. The in-class callouts give you specific moments to drop a one-liner like "by the way, this exact tactic also applies to ___" — keeps the class focused on Instagram while making it feel comprehensive.
01
Opening Framing
Use within the first 90 seconds — sets context before slide 1
▸ The opening script

"Why we're focused on Instagram today"

"Quick note before we dive in — today's class focuses heavily on Instagram and Facebook, and there's a reason. [pause] Instagram's interest-based algorithm is currently the strongest organic discovery engine for real estate — a brand-new agent can reach tens of thousands of people with one good reel. Facebook shares the same backend, so almost everything we cover today applies to FB Reels and Pages with zero translation."

"The other platforms — YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn — they all matter, and a lot of what we cover today still applies. I'll call those out as we go. But trying to cover five platforms in one hour means going shallow on all of them. So we're going deep on the one that's currently moving the most pipeline. Pick the platforms where your buyers actually are — for most DFW and Austin agents, that's Instagram and Facebook. YouTube is the long game. LinkedIn matters for luxury and referrals. TikTok if you're chasing first-time buyers under 35."

"Everything we cover — hooks, content pillars, the production system — translates. The DM mechanics are Meta-specific. Let's go."

Anticipated Q&A
Likely platform-related questions during Q&A
"Should I be on TikTok?"
"Depends on your audience. If you work with first-time buyers, especially under 35, yes — they're there. If you're targeting move-up buyers, luxury, or 50+ sellers, your time is better spent doubling down on Instagram and Facebook. Don't be on TikTok just because it's trendy. Be there if your buyers are."
"What about YouTube?"
"YouTube is the long game. Top-producing teams now have 'YouTube lead' as its own CRM source because those leads convert like referrals — they've already watched 20 minutes of you before they reach out. But it's a 6-to-12-month investment before pipeline shows up. If you have the patience, it's the highest-quality lead source on this list. Start with Shorts repurposed from your reels, then add long-form when you're ready."
"Does any of this work on LinkedIn?"
"For most agents, no — your buyers aren't there. For luxury agents and agents who want referral business from professionals, business owners, and executives — absolutely. The hooks work, the pillars work, the captions rule applies. The DM mechanics are different — no automation — but a single LinkedIn message thread is often worth ten Instagram ones for high-value clients. If you want luxury, get on LinkedIn."
"Can I just post the same thing everywhere?"
"Same video, yes — strip platform watermarks first. Same caption, no — each platform reads captions differently. Five seconds of editing per platform: change the first sentence to fit the audience tone, swap a couple keywords. The repurpose pipeline I showed you assumes you're doing this. It's still a fraction of the effort of filming five different videos."
"Which platform should I start with?"
"Pick the one where your existing sphere already is. For 90% of DFW and Austin agents, that's Facebook for the warm sphere and Instagram for new audience growth. Master those two — three Reels a week, daily Stories, comment-to-DM running — for 90 days before you add anything else. Most agents fail by trying to be on five platforms at once and being mediocre on all of them."
