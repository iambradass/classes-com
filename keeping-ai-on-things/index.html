The 5 Prompts

Copy any prompt, paste it into whatever AI assistant you use, and replace the [bracketed] parts with your details. That's it.

1  One Listing → A Full Campaign

Turn a single listing into a sphere email, three social captions, and an open-house angle, all in one pass.

You are my real estate marketing assistant. Create a complete launch package for this new listing.

LISTING:
- Address / area: [neighborhood or street, city]
- Price: [$]
- Beds / baths / size: [e.g. 4 bed / 3.5 bath / 4,200 sqft]
- 2-3 standout features: [e.g. resort pool, chef's kitchen, quiet cul-de-sac]
- One lifestyle hook: [e.g. walkable to the village, entertainer's backyard]
- My voice: [warm and personal / polished and concise / high-energy]

Produce, clearly labeled:
1. A short "just listed" email to my database (subject line + 120-150 words), ending with a soft "know someone who'd love it?" ask.
2. Three social captions with different angles: feature-forward, lifestyle, and scarcity. Add 3-5 relevant hashtags each.
3. An open-house angle: a one-line hook, three talking points, and one question that starts a conversation with every buyer who walks in.

Keep fair-housing safe: describe the home and lifestyle, never the ideal buyer or neighborhood demographics.

2  Conversation → Client Dossier + Tasks

Paste messy consult notes or a voice-note transcript and get a clean, one-page client record plus a next-action task list.

You are my executive assistant. Below are my raw notes / transcript from a client conversation. Turn them into a clean, one-page dossier I can attach to their record.

CONVERSATION:
[paste your messy notes or voice-note transcript here]

Extract and label clearly:
1. Must-haves (non-negotiable requirements)
2. Nice-to-haves (don't filter on these)
3. Dealbreakers (what eliminates a property)
4. Timeline + key dates
5. Financing context (budget, pre-approval, lender)
6. Privacy / communication preferences
7. Lifestyle & family details worth remembering
8. Open questions I still need to answer

Then give me a NEXT-ACTION TASK LIST: each task with an owner (me / lender / client) and a deadline.

Only use what's in the notes. If something wasn't mentioned, mark it "not discussed" - do not guess.

3  Next-Best-Action Queue

Decide who to reach out to, when, and how, without staring at your whole database.

You are my business-development assistant. Below is a list of contacts from my CRM (client database). For each one I've included the last time we talked, their role, and one recent note.

CONTACTS:
[paste 10-20 rows: name | role (past client / referral partner / active lead) | last contact date | one recent note]

For each contact, recommend:
1. The single next action (call, text, email, drop-by, handwritten note)
2. Why now (the trigger)
3. A suggested timeframe (today / this week / this month)

Then rank the top 5 I should do FIRST and explain the ranking in one line each.
Do not write the messages - just the action plan. I'll decide what to say.

4  Neighborhood Market-Update Post

Turn a few local numbers into a value post + sphere email that positions you as the area expert.

You are my content assistant. Turn these local market numbers into a value-first update for my audience - not a sales pitch.

AREA: [neighborhood / zip]
NUMBERS (most recent I have):
- Median sale price + year-over-year change: [ ]
- Active listings / months of supply: [ ]
- Average days on market: [ ]
- One notable trend: [e.g. cash buyers, new construction, low inventory]

Produce:
1. A short social post (100-130 words) that explains what these numbers mean for a normal buyer or seller, in plain English. One clear takeaway.
2. A matching email to my database with a subject line and a soft call to action ("reply if you want the full picture for your street").

Neutral, factual, fair-housing safe. Cite the numbers as "recent local data," and remind me to double-check figures before posting.

5  Weekly Transaction Status Brief

Turn a deal's moving parts into a calm client update and an internal checklist, so nothing slips.

You are my transaction coordinator. Based on the deal details below, write two things.

DEAL:
- Property / client: [ ]
- Contract date & closing date: [ ]
- Key upcoming deadlines: [option period, financing, appraisal, inspection, etc.]
- What's done / what's pending: [ ]
- Any issues or risks: [ ]

Produce:
1. A CLIENT-FACING weekly update (short, reassuring, plain English): what happened this week, what's next, anything I need from them.
2. An INTERNAL action checklist for me: every open item with a deadline and who owns it, flagging anything at risk of being late.

Keep the client version free of jargon. Keep the internal version blunt.

The Two Demo Recipes

The exact steps from today, so you can repeat them cold.

Recipe A: One Listing to a Full Campaign

  1. Open your AI assistant. Copy Prompt 1 above.
  2. Fill the brackets with one real listing (yours or a colleague's).
  3. Run it. Read the email, the three captions, and the open-house angle.
  4. Edit to sound like you. AI gets you 80% there, your voice does the rest.
  5. Send the email to your sphere and post one caption today. Done beats perfect.

Recipe B: A Conversation to a Client Dossier

  1. Right after a call or showing, dump your rough notes (or record a 60-second voice memo and transcribe it).
  2. Copy Prompt 2 and paste your notes into the brackets.
  3. Run it. You'll get a one-page dossier plus a task list.
  4. Scan it for accuracy and fix anything the AI misread.
  5. Attach the dossier to the client's record and drop the tasks into your CRM. Never lose a detail again.

A Starter Tool List

Organized by the job, not the brand. Pick one per row and learn it well before adding more.

General AI assistant

Your everyday chat tool for the prompts above. Popular options: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot.

Meeting / call notes

Records and transcribes calls and showings so you can feed them to Prompt 2. Options: Otter, Fathom, Fireflies, or your phone's voice memos.

Social scheduling

Queues the captions you generate so posting is one weekly task. Options: Later, Buffer, Metricool, or your CRM's built-in scheduler.

Your CRM (client database)

The home base for dossiers, tasks, and follow-up. Use the one you already have. The point is to actually use it.

These are examples to get you started, not endorsements. Any tool in each category works. Use what fits your business and budget.

Four Guardrails

The rules that keep AI helping you instead of exposing you.

  • Fair Housing applies to AI. HUD has confirmed the Fair Housing Act covers AI-driven ad targeting. Describe the home, never the "ideal buyer." Keep targeting broad and documented.
  • Never feed it private data. No Social Security numbers, full contracts, bank statements, or NDA-covered details in consumer AI. Sanitize before you paste.
  • Human review, every time. AI drafts and surfaces; you decide and send. Read every word before it reaches a client.
  • Keep your claims honest. Don't promise "AI-perfect pricing." Promise process, transparency, and accountability. You own the output, mistakes included.
