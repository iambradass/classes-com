The prompts

The Channel MapRun once; becomes a reference document in your workspace
channel-map.prompt
Interview me about my marketing channels, one at a time: email (what tool, list size, how often I send), text (from what system, who's opted in), social (which platforms I actually post on), landing pages (how I build them), print/mail (if any).

Then write my CHANNEL MAP: a short reference document listing each channel, its tool, its audience, its format constraints (character limits, image sizes), and its role (nurture, announce, capture). I'll save this in my workspace so every future campaign is drafted to fit my real channels.
The Campaign KickoffTrigger in, full package out
campaign-kickoff.prompt
Using my Agent Profile and my Channel Map, build a complete campaign.

TRIGGER: [what's this campaign about: new listing / just sold / market update / event / seasonal]
DETAILS: [the facts: property, numbers with sources, dates]
AUDIENCE: [whole database / a segment / the farm]
GOAL: [conversations started / showings booked / home-value requests]
DURATION: [e.g. 2 weeks]

Produce the full package, organized by channel from my Channel Map:
1. Every asset, final-draft quality, in my voice, formatted to each channel's constraints.
2. A calendar: what goes out on which day, in which channel, in what order, and why that sequence.
3. A load list: the exact steps to get each asset into each tool, in order, so I can load the whole campaign in one sitting.
4. A measurement plan: the 3 numbers to check in two weeks and where I'll find them.

Fair-housing safe throughout. Flag any claim in my DETAILS that needs a source or a double-check before it ships.
The DebriefResults in, one compounding improvement out
debrief.prompt
The campaign ran. Here are the results from my tools: [paste opens, clicks, replies, form fills, conversations, and anything anecdotal]

Tell me: what worked, what underperformed, the single highest-impact change for next time, and rewrite the one weakest asset to show me the fix. Then give me the updated line to add to my Channel Map or Agent Profile so the improvement sticks.

Watch-outs

Two checkpoints, always. You review the full package before loading, and the schedule before launch. Automation earns trust; it never inherits it.

Consent lives in your tools. Email and especially text campaigns go only to people with proper opt-in, and your sending tools enforce unsubscribes. The assistant never sends directly.

One campaign at a time until the debrief loop works. The failure mode of automation isn't a bad email; it's ten mediocre campaigns nobody measured.

Advertising rules ride along: broker identification, license disclosures, and your brokerage's ad policies apply to every asset in the package.
