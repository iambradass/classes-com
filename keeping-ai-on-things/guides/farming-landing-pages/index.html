You walk away with: one polished page about one neighborhood: what makes it special, a dated market snapshot, and a "what's my home worth" call to action wired to your CRM. A QR that opens a page about their street beats one that opens your generic homepage.

What you'll need

Real facts about the neighborhood: your recent MLS numbers, amenities, and what residents actually love about it.

The prompt

The Page BuilderOne complete, mobile-first HTML file
page-builder.prompt
You are my web developer and copywriter. Build me a complete, single-file HTML landing page for a neighborhood I farm. Everything (styles, scripts) inline in one file so I can preview it by opening the file in a browser.

NEIGHBORHOOD: [name, city]
WHAT RESIDENTS LOVE (my notes): [5 bullets: parks, schools zone info stated neutrally, walkability, architecture, community events]
MARKET SNAPSHOT (my MLS numbers, with dates): [median sale price, days on market, homes sold last quarter]
ABOUT ME: [name, brokerage, phone, email, one-line positioning]
LEAD CAPTURE: [paste your CRM form embed code, or say "placeholder form" and I'll wire it later]

Requirements:
- Mobile-first. Most visitors scan a QR code from print. Large text, big tap targets, fast load, no images I have to source (use color and typography instead; leave clearly-marked placeholder spots where I can add my own photos).
- Sections: hero with the neighborhood name and a one-line hook, "living here" highlights, market snapshot with a "numbers as of [date]" line, a home-value call to action with the lead form, and a short about-me footer.
- Tone: warm local expert, zero hype. This page is about the neighborhood, not about me.
- Fair-housing safe: describe the place, homes, and lifestyle. Never describe the people who live there or who "should" live there, and no demographic language of any kind.
- Include a title tag and meta description for search.

Give me the complete file, then a bulleted list of exactly what I should customize before publishing.

Watch-outs

Fair housing applies to farming pages twice: the page copy AND any advertising you run to promote it. Describe property and place, keep ad targeting broad, document what you ran.

Source your numbers. Every market stat on the page needs a date and a source you could show if asked. "Numbers as of June 2026, [your MLS]" is the habit.

Brokerage advertising rules still apply: required broker identification, license disclosures, and ad review policies cover this page like any other ad.
