AI Transaction Coordination

Feed your assistant the contract dates once. Get the full timeline, weekly client updates, and an at-risk radar for the life of the deal.

You walk away with: a full contract-to-close timeline (option period, financing, appraisal, title commitment and objection windows, HOA documents, closing), a weekly two-version update, and early flags on whatever is quietly at risk.

The timeline updates and the next brief reflects reality. The deal chat becomes the deal's memory.

The prompts

The Timeline BuilderDates in, master timeline + critical three + week-one checklist out
timeline-builder.prompt
You are my transaction coordinator's assistant for this deal. I will give you the dates from the executed contract; you organize, you never invent.

DEAL:
- Property / parties / my side (listing or buyer): [ ]
- Effective date: [ ]  Closing date: [ ]
- Option or inspection period ends: [ ]
- Financing deadline: [ ]  Appraisal ordered/due: [ ]
- Title commitment due: [ ]  Objection deadline: [ ]
- HOA documents due (if any): [ ]
- Other negotiated deadlines: [ ]

Produce:
1. MASTER TIMELINE: every deadline in date order, with what must be TRUE by that date, who owns it (me / my client / lender / title company / other agent), and the date I should check in (2-3 days before each deadline, adjusted for weekends).
2. THE CRITICAL THREE: which three dates on this deal have the least slack, and why.
3. WEEK ONE CHECKLIST: everything that should happen in the next 7 days.

If any date I gave you conflicts with another (for example a deadline after closing), stop and flag it before producing the timeline.
The Weekly BriefA calm client version and a blunt internal version
weekly-brief.prompt
Using this deal's timeline, here's what happened this week: [2-5 bullets: done, pending, new issues]

Produce:
1. CLIENT VERSION: a short, reassuring, plain-English update. What happened, what's next, exactly what (if anything) I need from them. No jargon without a translation.
2. MY VERSION: every open item with a deadline and an owner, anything now at risk of slipping flagged at the top, and the one phone call I should make Monday morning.

Watch-outs

The contract is the only calendar that counts. Verify every AI-organized date against the executed documents.

This assists coordination; it isn't a TC (transaction coordinator). Your broker's supervision requirements and your TC's process don't change because a chat window got involved.

Watch what you paste. Deal terms are fine in a training-off business account; buyers' financial documents are never fine. Dates and statuses, not bank statements.

Escalating issues need humans. Appraisal gap, title issue, repair standoff: the AI helps you think; the phone call is you. Your title company's escrow team has seen it all before. Use them.
