Market Research Routines

Recurring AI tasks that watch your market areas while you sell, and hand you a digest worth reading.

Level 3 · Autopilot · 30 minutes to set up

What this does

  • Runs on a schedule you set. Several major assistants now support scheduled or recurring tasks that execute without you opening the app (check your assistant's features for "tasks," "scheduled actions," or similar).
  • Weekly: a digest of what's moving in your areas: notable new listings, price reductions, momentum shifts, and local news that touches real estate (development, schools, employers).
  • Monthly: competitor awareness. Who's listing in your farm, what marketing is showing up, where the market share is moving.
  • Quarterly: an opportunity scan. Where the data suggests your next farm, niche, or content angle.

What you'll need

  • An assistant tier with scheduled tasks and web browsing. If yours has browsing but not scheduling, run the same prompts manually with a recurring calendar reminder; same value, one extra tap.
  • A clear definition of "your market": 2-4 named neighborhoods or zip codes. Vague areas make vague research.

The setup

  1. Find the scheduling feature in your assistant (search its help for "scheduled tasks" or "recurring"). Create a new task, set it to weekly, and pick the morning you do your business planning.
  2. Paste the Weekly Watch prompt below as the task's instruction, filled in with your areas.
  3. Let it run twice, then tighten it. The first reports will be too broad. Reply with "less of X, more of Y" and edit the task. By week three it's yours.
  4. Add the Monthly Competitor Scan as a second task on the first of the month.
  5. Turn digests into action: the point isn't reading, it's the one call, post, or postcard each digest suggests. If a digest doesn't change what you do that week, tighten the prompt again.

The prompts

The Weekly Watch

You are my market analyst. Every week, research what changed in my market areas and send me a digest.

MY AREAS: [2-4 neighborhoods or zips, city]
MY BUSINESS: [listings-focused / buyers / both, price range]

Research publicly available sources (portals, local news, city/county announcements) and report:
1. MARKET MOVEMENT: notable new listings, price cuts, and anything unusual (very fast sale, big price, long sit) in my areas this week.
2. LOCAL SIGNALS: news that touches property values or moving decisions: development, road projects, school changes, major employers.
3. ONE OPPORTUNITY: the single most actionable thing in this digest for an agent in my position, and the concrete action to take this week.

Rules: link every claim to its source. If you can't verify something, label it "unverified." Never present an estimate as MLS data. Keep the whole digest under 400 words.

The Monthly Competitor Scan

Once a month, scan publicly visible activity in my market areas: [areas].

Report: which agents/teams listed or sold there this month (from public portal data), what marketing of theirs is visible (content, mailers people mention, events), and any new player showing up repeatedly.

Then the part that matters: 2-3 specific gaps. What is nobody serving in these areas (a content angle, a price band, a seller situation) that I could own? Sources linked, under 300 words, no speculation about anyone's business beyond what's publicly visible.

Watch-outs

  • Web data is not MLS data. Portal numbers lag and estimate. Before any stat from a digest reaches a client or a post, verify it in the MLS. The digest tells you where to look, not what to say.
  • Scheduled tasks drift. Skim critically for the first month. An unwatched routine slowly becomes a confident newsletter of stale information.
  • Competitor research stays professional. Public activity only. You're mapping the market, not surveilling colleagues.
