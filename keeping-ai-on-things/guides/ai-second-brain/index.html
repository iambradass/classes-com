Your AI Second Brain

Teach your assistant who you are once, so every draft sounds like you and every other playbook works better.

30 minutes, once Any AI assistant

Why this is play zero

What this does

Ends the generic AI voice. Your assistant learns your market, voice, and rules one time instead of you re-explaining them in every chat.

Creates a permanent workspace (a "Project," "Gem," or custom assistant, depending on your tool) where all your business work happens.

Powers every other play. Each guide on the hub assumes this exists. Do this one first.

What you'll need

A paid account on the AI assistant you already use. Examples, not endorsements: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini. The paid tiers add persistent workspaces and better privacy controls.

15 minutes of honest answers about your business.

The setup

  1. 1

    Turn off training

    In your assistant's settings, find the data or privacy controls and turn off "improve the model with my data" (the wording varies by tool). Business accounts usually default to off. Do this before anything else.

  2. 2

    Run the interview prompt

    The AI interviews you, then writes your Agent Profile: a one-page document describing your business, voice, and rules. The prompt is below.

  3. 3

    Read the profile and fix what's wrong

    This document is the foundation. If it says you're "high-energy" and you're not, every future draft will be off.

  4. 4

    Create your workspace

    ChatGPT calls it a Project or custom GPT, Claude calls it a Project, Gemini calls it a Gem. Create one named "My Real Estate Business" and paste the Agent Profile into its instructions or knowledge area.

  5. 5

    Test it

    Inside the workspace, ask for a short sphere email about staying in touch over the summer. If it sounds like you, you're done. If not, edit the profile and test again.

  6. 6

    Refresh it quarterly

    Markets and goals change. Put a calendar reminder to re-read the profile every quarter.

What "sounds like you" looks like

The test, on your phone

Step 5 is the whole point. Ask for something small you'd actually send, and judge one thing only: would your past clients know it wasn't you? Keep editing the profile until the answer is no.

The prompts

agent-profile-interview.prompt

Run this in a fresh chat. Answer its questions, then it writes your profile.

You are helping me build my "Agent Profile" - a one-page document that will live in my AI workspace so every future draft matches my business and voice.

Interview me one question at a time. Cover:
1. My market: the areas and price points I actually work, and the ones I'm trying to grow.
2. My clients: past clients, sphere, referral partners - who my business really comes from.
3. My voice: ask me to paste 2-3 things I've written that sound like me (emails, posts, texts). Analyze the tone and describe it in plain words.
4. My rules: things I never say, words I hate, formats I prefer, how formal I am by channel (text vs email vs social).
5. My standing guardrails: fair-housing safe language always (describe homes and lifestyle, never the "ideal buyer"), no private client data, and I review everything before it goes out.

When the interview is done, write the Agent Profile as a clean one-page document with these headings: Market, Clients, Voice, Rules, Guardrails. Keep it under 400 words so it's easy to maintain.
voice-check.prompt

Use this any time a draft doesn't sound like you.

Compare this draft against my Agent Profile. List every line that doesn't sound like me and rewrite just those lines in my voice. Then tell me in one sentence what pattern you keep getting wrong, so I can add a rule to my profile.

DRAFT:
[paste the draft]

Watch-outs

No client private data in the profile. The profile describes your business, not your clients. Names, addresses, and deal details stay out.

One profile, one home. Keep the master copy in a document you own (notes app, drive). Paste it into workspaces. Don't let five slightly different versions drift apart.

Your brokerage may have an AI policy. Check it before you connect tools or upload data. Five minutes now saves a hard conversation later.
