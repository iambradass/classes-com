The prompts

The Agent Profile InterviewRun in a fresh chat; it asks, you answer, it writes your profile
agent-profile-interview.prompt
You are helping me build my "Agent Profile" - a one-page document that will live in my AI workspace so every future draft matches my business and voice.

Interview me one question at a time. Cover:
1. My market: the areas and price points I actually work, and the ones I'm trying to grow.
2. My clients: past clients, sphere, referral partners - who my business really comes from.
3. My voice: ask me to paste 2-3 things I've written that sound like me (emails, posts, texts). Analyze the tone and describe it in plain words.
4. My rules: things I never say, words I hate, formats I prefer, how formal I am by channel (text vs email vs social).
5. My standing guardrails: fair-housing safe language always (describe homes and lifestyle, never the "ideal buyer"), no private client data, and I review everything before it goes out.

When the interview is done, write the Agent Profile as a clean one-page document with these headings: Market, Clients, Voice, Rules, Guardrails. Keep it under 400 words so it's easy to maintain.
The Voice CheckUse any time a draft doesn't sound like you
voice-check.prompt
Compare this draft against my Agent Profile. List every line that doesn't sound like me and rewrite just those lines in my voice. Then tell me in one sentence what pattern you keep getting wrong, so I can add a rule to my profile.

DRAFT:
[paste the draft]

Watch-outs

No client private data in the profile. The profile describes your business, not your clients. Names, addresses, and deal details stay out.

One profile, one home. Keep the master copy in a document you own (notes app, drive). Paste it into workspaces. Don't let five slightly different versions drift apart.

Your brokerage may have an AI policy. Check it before you connect tools or upload data. Five minutes now saves a hard conversation later.
