The Reverse Prospecting Engine

Your MLS (Multiple Listing Service) already tells you which agents have buyers matching your listing. One copy-paste turns that into a full campaign.

20 min per listing Real demand data

What this does

Agent outreach, one by one, in your voice. The MLS report shows whose buyer searches match your listing; the engine writes the message to each of them.

The seller update that proves you're marketing: "X agents have active buyers matching our home, here's what I'm doing about it."

A proof-of-demand campaign for your channels: sphere email, social posts, and an open-house angle, all keyed to real buyer demand instead of generic "just listed" noise.

What you'll need

An active listing and your MLS's reverse prospecting report for it. In most MLS systems it's on the listing management screen; your MLS help desk can point to it in 2 minutes.

Your AI Second Brain workspace.

The setup

  1. 1

    Run reverse prospecting in the MLS

    Copy the results: the count of matching buyer searches and, where shown, the agent names and search criteria.

  2. 2

    Strip anything marked confidential

    Counts and criteria are what the engine needs. When in doubt, paste the counts only.

  3. 3

    Run the Engine prompt

    Feed it your listing details and the pasted results.

  4. 4

    Send the agent outreach first

    Those are the warmest possible calls in real estate: their buyer is already looking for your listing.

  5. 5

    Send the seller update

    Nothing builds seller confidence like evidence of demand.

  6. 6

    Queue the public campaign

    Load it through your CRM or scheduler.

  7. 7

    Repeat weekly while active

    New matches appear as new buyers enter the market.

The warmest message in real estate

What the outreach looks like

Peer to peer, zero pressure, specific. The engine drafts a text and email version for every matching agent; you review and hit send. Most agents answer this message the same day, because it's about their buyer, not your listing.

The prompt

reverse-prospecting-engine.prompt
You are my listing marketing strategist. My MLS's reverse prospecting report shows real buyer demand for my listing. Build the full campaign.

LISTING:
- Address / area: [ ]
- Price, beds, baths, size: [ ]
- 2-3 standout features: [ ]

REVERSE PROSPECTING RESULTS (pasted from my MLS):
[paste the results: number of matching buyer searches, and agent names / search criteria if shown]

Produce, clearly labeled:
1. AGENT OUTREACH: a short, professional message to each matching agent (or a single template if I only pasted counts). Tone: peer to peer, zero pressure, "your buyer's criteria matches my new listing, happy to arrange a showing." Give me a text version and an email version.
2. SELLER UPDATE: an email to my seller reporting the demand signal and what I'm doing about it this week. Reassuring, specific, no jargon.
3. DEMAND CAMPAIGN: one sphere email and two social captions built around the demand story ("N buyer searches matched this home in its first week"). Fair-housing safe: describe the home and lifestyle, never the buyers.
4. FOLLOW-UP CADENCE: when I re-run this report and re-touch each audience over the next 21 days.

Use only the numbers I pasted. Do not inflate or invent demand.

Level it up

Save it as a template. Store the Engine prompt in your workspace so each new listing is fill-in-the-brackets, 5 minutes flat.

Connected version: with your CRM connected, have the assistant drop the follow-up cadence in as dated tasks so nothing depends on your memory.

Watch-outs

Contact the agent, never their buyer. Reverse prospecting data is for agent-to-agent outreach. Going around a buyer's agent is a fast way to a board complaint.

Know your MLS's rules for how reverse prospecting data can be used and shared. Quoting aggregate counts publicly ("14 matching buyer searches") is generally the safe zone; naming agents or criteria publicly is not.

Real numbers only. If demand is thin, the play still works: tell the seller the truth and let the AI help you build the plan to change it.
