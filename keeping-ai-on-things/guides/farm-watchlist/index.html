The Farm Watchlist

You already have the farm list. This play cross-references it with what actually happened in the neighborhood this month, and tells you who to talk to about it.

Level 3 · Autopilot · 30 minutes to set up, 15 a month to run

What this does

  • Compares your master farm list against this month's activity export (new listings, solds, expireds, withdrawn) and finds the intersections that matter.
  • Produces a ranked touch list: the neighbor of the house that just sold high, the expired two streets from your last closing, the long-time owner on a street where three homes just moved.
  • Gives every touch a reason. "The Hendersons' street just set a record" is a conversation. "Just checking in" is a delete.

What you'll need

  • Your master farm list as a CSV spreadsheet file: address, owner name if you have it, and any history column (past touches, responses). Keep one master file; update it, don't fork it.
  • A monthly activity export for the farm area from your MLS (Multiple Listing Service): actives, pendings, solds, expireds with addresses and prices.
  • Your AI Second Brain workspace, with the sanitize-before-upload habit from the CSV guide.

The setup

  1. Make it a monthly ritual: first Tuesday of the month, 15 minutes, calendar reminder titled "Run the farm watchlist."
  2. Export this month's activity for the farm boundaries from the MLS.
  3. Upload both files (master farm list + activity export) to your workspace and run the Cross-Reference prompt.
  4. Work the top ten. The output ranks touches by strength of reason. Ten real conversations beat a hundred generic postcards.
  5. Feed results back. Add a note to your master list for every touch made ("6/26: talked, thinking of selling next spring"). Next month's run gets smarter because the file remembers.
  6. Optional autopilot: if your assistant supports scheduled tasks, schedule the monthly reminder with the prompt pre-loaded so the run is one upload away.

The prompt

The Cross-Reference

You are my farm analyst. I've uploaded two files:
1. MASTER FARM LIST: the homes/owners in my geographic farm, with my touch history.
2. THIS MONTH'S ACTIVITY: MLS activity in the same area (actives, pendings, solds, expireds).

Cross-reference them:
1. DIRECT HITS: any address on my farm list that appears in this month's activity. What happened, and what it means for my relationship with that address.
2. RIPPLE TOUCHES: farm addresses on the same street or within a couple of blocks of a notable event (a strong sale, a fast sale, an expired). For each, the specific conversation opener the event creates. Fair-housing safe: talk about homes, prices, and the market, never about who is moving in or out.
3. THE RANKED TEN: the 10 best touches this month, ordered by strength of reason, each with: address/name, the reason, the channel (door knock, call, note, mailer), and a 1-2 sentence opener in my voice.
4. LIST HEALTH: anything in my master list that looks stale or inconsistent (owner sold last year, duplicate rows), so I can clean it.

Match addresses carefully (abbreviations like St/Street, unit numbers). List any rows you couldn't confidently match instead of guessing.

Watch-outs

  • Verify the trigger before you knock. "I saw the home on Maple sold" had better be true. Check the MLS record before you lead with it.
  • Expireds and withdrawns have rules. Solicitation of expired listings is regulated and brokerage-policy-sensitive. Know your rules before that touch.
  • Owner data comes with obligations. If your farm list came from a data provider, its license says what you can do with it. Uploading to your training-off business assistant for your own analysis is the conservative pattern; republishing is not.
  • Ripple touches never speculate about people. The market event is the opener. Who's moving, why they left, what they got: not yours to discuss.
